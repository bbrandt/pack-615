If you are a boy in the first through fifth grade then Cub Scouting is for you.  Cub Scouting is a family activity.  As a Cub Scout, you are a member of a pack.  The pack is divided into smaller groups called dens.  A den is usually 6 to 8 boys of the same rank (age). 


Cub Scouting is all about doing.  Much of the action happens in the den or pack meeting, and the rest is with your family.  Dens usually meet every week and the pack meets once a month. 


The pack also has annual events such as the Rain Gutter Regatta, Pinewood Derby,  Blue and Gold Banquet, Pack Campouts, and summertime activities.


While you are having fun and learning you will also be earning awards.  You will receive your awards at the monthly pack meeting in a ceremony in front of the pack. 


The aim of Cub Scouting is to help boys grow into good citizens who are strong in character and personally fit. 


Scouting is fun with a purpose.
